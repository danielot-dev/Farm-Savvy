Best wheat varieties for clay soil?

Rajesh Kumar
Posted 2 hours ago
Crop Production

Hello everyone,

I have about 5 acres of clay soil in Punjab (India) and I'm planning to grow wheat this coming season. The soil retains moisture well but can be slow to drain and gets compacted easily.

I'm looking for recommendations on wheat varieties that perform well in these conditions. Specifically:

  • Varieties with good root systems that can handle heavier soils
  • Disease-resistant varieties (we've had issues with rust in the past)
  • Good yield potential (targeting at least 4 tons/hectare)

I've grown PBW 550 in the past with moderate success, but I'm wondering if there are better options. Any advice from farmers with similar soil conditions would be greatly appreciated!

Thanks in advance,
Rajesh

Rajesh, I've farmed clay soils in Punjab for 15 years. For your conditions, I recommend:

  1. HD 2967: Excellent for heavy soils, good rust resistance, yields 4.5-5 t/ha with proper management
  2. WH 1105: Performs well in clay, tolerant to waterlogging, consistent 4-4.5 t/ha
  3. PBW 725: Newer variety with good disease package, needs slightly better drainage

Key tips for clay soils:

  • Deep plowing (25-30 cm) before planting to improve root penetration
  • Add organic matter (5-10 tons/acre of farmyard manure) to improve structure
  • Consider raised beds if drainage is very poor

I second HD 2967 for clay soils. We've been growing it for 3 years now on our farm near Ludhiana with similar soil conditions. Consistently getting 4.2-4.8 t/ha with proper irrigation and fertilizer.

One thing to watch for - it's somewhat susceptible to Karnal bunt if you have history of that in your area.

From a research perspective, we've found these varieties perform well in clay soils in recent trials at PAU:

Variety Clay Soil Yield Disease Resistance
PBW 752 4.6 t/ha Good for rust, moderate for bunt
DBW 187 4.8 t/ha Excellent all-around

Important to get your soil tested - clay soils in Punjab often need additional zinc supplementation.
