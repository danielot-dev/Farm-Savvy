Cattle Farming

Cattle are among the most important domesticated animals, providing milk, meat, leather, and draft power. Proper cattle management can lead to increased productivity and profitability.

Quick Facts

Scientific Name: Bos taurus
Gestation Period: 9 months (283 days)
Lifespan: 18-22 years (dairy), 10-12 years (beef)
Milk Production: 6,000-12,000 kg/year (dairy breeds)
Market Weight: 500-700 kg (beef cattle at 18-24 months)

Cattle Farming Guide

Types of Cattle Farming

There are two main types of cattle farming systems:

  • Dairy Farming: Focused on milk production. Requires specialized breeds like Holstein, Jersey, or Brown Swiss.
  • Beef Farming: Focused on meat production. Common breeds include Angus, Hereford, and Charolais.

Housing Requirements

Proper housing is essential for cattle health and productivity:

  • Space: 30-40 sq ft per adult animal in covered area
  • Ventilation: Good air circulation to prevent respiratory diseases
  • Flooring: Non-slip, well-drained surfaces
  • Feeding area: Separate space for feeding with adequate trough space
  • Milking parlor: For dairy farms - clean, organized milking area

Common Breeds

Holstein-Friesian

Type: Dairy
Characteristics: High milk yield (6,000-12,000 kg/year), black and white coloration
Origin: Netherlands

Jersey

Type: Dairy
Characteristics: High butterfat content (4.9%), light brown color, docile temperament
Origin: Jersey Island

Angus

Type: Beef
Characteristics: Excellent meat quality, naturally polled (no horns), black or red color
Origin: Scotland

Nutrition Requirements

Cattle require a balanced diet for optimal health and production:

Animal Type Dry Matter Intake Protein Requirement Energy Requirement
Dairy Cow (producing) 3-4% of body weight 16-18% of diet High (6.5-7.5 Mcal/kg)
Beef Cattle (growing) 2.5-3% of body weight 12-14% of diet Moderate (5.5-6.5 Mcal/kg)
Pregnant Cow 2-2.5% of body weight 10-12% of diet Moderate (5.0-6.0 Mcal/kg)

Feed should consist of good quality forage (hay, silage) supplemented with concentrates (grains, protein meals) as needed.

Health Management

Common cattle diseases and prevention:

Disease Symptoms Prevention/Treatment
Mastitis Inflamed udder, abnormal milk Clean milking practices, antibiotics
Foot Rot Lameness, swollen foot Clean dry environment, foot baths
Bovine Respiratory Disease Coughing, nasal discharge, fever Vaccination, good ventilation

Breeding Management

Key aspects of cattle reproduction:

  • Puberty: 6-12 months (heifers), 9-15 months (bulls)
  • Estrous cycle: 21 days (heat lasts 12-18 hours)
  • Breeding methods: Natural mating or artificial insemination
  • Pregnancy diagnosis: Rectal palpation (35+ days) or ultrasound (28+ days)

Milk Production (Dairy)

Factors affecting milk yield:

  • Breed: Specialized dairy breeds produce more milk
  • Nutrition: Balanced diet with adequate energy and protein
  • Milking frequency: 2-3 times daily increases yield
  • Lactation stage: Peak production at 6-8 weeks postpartum
  • Environment: Comfortable conditions reduce stress

Beef Production

Beef production systems:

  • Cow-calf: Breeding herd producing calves for sale
  • Stocker: Growing calves on pasture
  • Feedlot: Intensive finishing on high-energy diets

Average daily gain targets:

  • Pasture: 0.7-1.0 kg/day
  • Feedlot: 1.2-1.8 kg/day
