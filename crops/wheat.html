Wheat

Triticum aestivum

Wheat is one of the world's most important cereal crops, providing about 20% of the global population's food energy intake. It's versatile, nutritious, and can be grown in various climates.

Quick Facts

Family: Poaceae
Growth Period: 3-8 months
Optimal Temperature: 15-20°C
Soil pH: 6.0-7.5
Water Needs: Moderate (500-800mm)

Growing Guide

Climate Requirements

Wheat grows best in temperate regions with cool, moist weather during the growing season and warm, dry weather during harvest. It can tolerate temperatures as low as -20°C when properly hardened.

Soil Preparation

Wheat prefers well-drained loamy soils with good organic matter content. Prepare the field by:

  • Plowing to a depth of 15-20 cm
  • Harrowing to break up clods
  • Leveling the field for uniform irrigation
  • Adding compost or manure (2-3 tons per acre)

Planting

Optimal planting times vary by region and wheat type:

Wheat Type Planting Season Seeding Rate (kg/ha)
Winter Wheat Autumn (before first frost) 100-150
Spring Wheat Early Spring 120-180

Plant seeds 2-5 cm deep in rows spaced 15-20 cm apart.

Irrigation

Wheat requires about 500-800mm of water during its growing season. Critical irrigation stages:

  • Crown root initiation (20-25 days after planting)
  • Flowering stage
  • Grain filling stage

Fertilization

Recommended fertilizer application (per acre):

  • Nitrogen (N): 50-100 kg (split application)
  • Phosphorus (P₂O₅): 30-60 kg (at planting)
  • Potassium (K₂O): 20-40 kg (for deficient soils)

Common Varieties

Hard Red Winter

High protein content, excellent for bread flour. Grown in central and southern plains.

Soft Red Winter

Lower protein, used for cakes and pastries. Common in eastern states.

Durum

Very hard wheat used for pasta. Grown in northern plains.

Pest and Disease Management

Common wheat problems and solutions:

Problem Symptoms Solution
Rust Orange-brown pustules on leaves Resistant varieties, fungicides
Aphids Curled leaves, sticky residue Natural predators, insecticidal soap
Fusarium Head Blight Bleached spikelets Crop rotation, fungicides

Harvesting

Wheat is ready for harvest when:

  • Kernels are hard and dry (moisture content 13-15%)
  • Straw turns golden brown
  • Kernels can't be dented with a thumbnail

Use a combine harvester or traditional methods like sickles for small plots.

Yield Expectations

Average yields vary by region and farming practices:

  • Rainfed: 1-3 tons per hectare
  • Irrigated: 3-6 tons per hectare
  • High-input systems: 6-10 tons per hectare
